Make Your Mark

"I just want the world to know I was here, that I matter." (Cyberpunk 2077)

Nostalgia: a sentimental longing or wistful affection for the past, typically for a period or place with happy personal associations. The proper way to say the “warm fuzzy feelings” one might get when rewatching their favorite movie or remembering a fun time with their friends in high school. Nostalgia and human memory are powerful things. But with that power can come a fear of being snuffed out. That once you’re gone, you may not be remembered by anyone.

Ernest Hemingway once said, “Every man has two deaths, when he is buried in the ground and the last time someone says his name.” Many seek to leave a mark on this world, so that even after they are buried in the ground, people may yet say their name. Writing on the walls of a house as it’s being built, signing your name on a drawing you just finished, or the simple act of carving your name in a tree. All to say, “I was here!”

And what do these monoliths evoke in their observers? Secondhand nostalgia. One almost feels for the times that were carved into that rock face. Why was that person so angry? Are those two names still in love?

Eventually, someone will be looking at those rocks like artifacts, names scratched into fossilized stone the same way we now look at ancient manuscripts of shopping lists, or Viking runes carved into the walls of ancient caves.

At the end of the day, no one may remember your name, but maybe a small mark is all it takes to say, “I was here!” and let someone know you existed.
